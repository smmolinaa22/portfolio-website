About

My Story

Hi there! My name is Sarah Molina, and I am a UX designer and occupational therapist (OT). I grew my passions for helping others, finding creative solutions to complex problems, and empowering both children and adults through my occupational therapy background. In the field of OT, we help people to complete activities that may be challenging for them, i.e.,

  • an elderly woman with arthritis having difficulty opening her medication bottles
  • a child with CP having challenges playing with his friends due to motor and physical differences
  • or a teenager with a progressive visual impairment wanting to learn to play the piano
by analyzing personal factors (i.e. motivations, needs, physical and cognitive limitations), environmental factors, and the occupation (what they are trying to accomplish). Through finding innovative and personalized solutions, we can make their experiences easier, more meaningful, memorable, and valuable. Through OT, I grew my critical thinking, problem-solving, empathy, and understanding of peopleâ€™s needs, motivations, and behaviors from a broad and diverse population. However, I still wanted to grow in the areas of design, making designs accessible for all. With OT, sessions are typically 1:1; with technology, several lives can be impacted with just one good design. My UX design journey has consisted of:
  • empathizing with users, looking through their lens to discover problems and pain points when completing certain tasks
  • finding solutions to these problems
  • and using iterative design, testing, and research to solidify these solutions.
With my diverse background of understanding human psychology & motivations, accessibility, inclusion, and task analysis, I aim to create human-centered and accessibility-centered designs for all to enjoy. Download my resume
