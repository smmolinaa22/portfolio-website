Waves UX Design Case Study

Context: CareerFoundry UX Design Certificate
Roles: UX Design, User Research, Information Architecture
About: Waves is a water mobile app created to connect people with easy to understand wind, wave, and weather information for quick decision making on their water days.

The Process

Research

Who are we designing for and what do they need? We completed a competitive analysis, SWOT analysis, and user surveys to discover more.

Key Research Insights

Users needed a mobile app to access water weather conditions to enjoy their leisure water activities.

When completing our research, we learned:

  1. No apps on the market currently cater to leisurely water goers (discovered via competitive analysis and SWOT)
  2. Majority of participants surveyed reported their primary purpose during water activities is for leisure (discovered via surveys)

POV

Using the information and perspective gained from user interviews, we created personas to represent members of our target audience. These personas helped us stay user-centered throughout our design process.

Ideation

  • user journey mapping
  • creating user flows
  • creating and refining our sitemap

User Journey Mapping

User journey mapping helped us to understand the thoughts, feelings, and emotions of our personas when completing tasks.

By empathizing with our users, we learned that the app needs to:

  • provide quick, accurate, and detailed weather and water conditions
  • have trustworthy information from other users (i.e., reviews and pictures)
  • have advanced filters so users can find the locations they want.

Creating User Flows and Refining Our Sitemap

We started creating user flows to visualize the steps of a task our user personas would take when using the app.

We then began expanding our product’s information architecture, creating a sitemap, which gave structure and organization to our ideas.

Usability Testing

After completing usability testing with our clickable prototype, we identified the top 5 issues our participants were having. We then identified and implemented solutions to address these issues.

1. Confusion with the hamburger menu function

Solution: Change hamburger menu to a settings menu with radio buttons or show/hide options within the settings page.

2. Scrolling up/down is not obvious on scrollable pages

Solution: Place a scroll bar/scroll indicator on the right side of the page for scrollable pages, specifically, the home and location pages.

3. Redundant buttons (i.e. filter icon and “more filters” drop down) may cause confusion with navigation

Solution: Change the current filter icon to a list view icon; keep the “more filters” drop down the same.

4. Prototype linking is not smooth when using the “applying filters” feature

Solution: Fix prototype linking in Figma to be more dynamic rather than linear. Some pages are not linked correctly and do not fully represent the Search/Filters features.

5. Users cannot see pictures when looking at reviews / users cannot see reviews

Solution: Place the user’s review under the enlarged picture / place thumbnails of pictures included with the review in the Reviews tab.

Reflection and Feedback

Developing Waves from start to finish was a rewarding and challenging process. I grew my strengths in using different design tools (i.e. Figma), completing thorough user research, collaborative design with peers, effectively managing time within a project, and prioritizing accessibility in design. Moving forward, I now have a more systematic way of approaching new problems. I will be learning more about accessibility in design, other design software, and collaboration with other disciplines to further my design growth and strengths.
